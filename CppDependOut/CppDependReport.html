Abstractness versus Instability Diagram

The Abstractness versus Instability Diagram helps to detect which Projects are potentially painful to maintain (i.e concrete and stable) and which Projects are potentially useless (i.e abstract and instable).

  • Abstractness: If an assembly contains many abstract types (i.e interfaces and abstract classes) and few concrete types, it is considered as abstract.
  • Stability: An assembly is considered stable if its types are used by a lot of types of tier Projects. In this conditions stable means painful to modify.
